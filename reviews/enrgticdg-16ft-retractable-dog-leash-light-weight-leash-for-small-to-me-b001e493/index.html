Frequently Asked Questions

Is the enrgticdg Retractable Leashes suitable for cats as well as dogs?

Yes, it works well for many cats and small dogs, especially those that respond to a single handed brake. The lighter housing and thinner tape are easier for small pets to pull on, but always pair it with a secure harness to avoid neck strain and improve control.

How do I prevent the tape from tangling?

Keep the tape clean and allow it to fully retract before storing to reduce twists. Lead the tape from a short distance rather than letting it wrap around objects, and check the spring action periodically to ensure smooth retraction that helps avoid coils.

What is the best way to clean the leash after muddy walks?

Wipe the tape with a damp cloth and mild soap, then let it retract slowly while drying to prevent moisture trapped inside the housing. Avoid soaking the entire unit, and check metal parts for rust so you can dry and lubricate clips if needed.

Are there safety tips for using the one-handed brake?

Practice the brake action in a quiet area before busy walks so you know how much pressure is needed for a quick stop. Keep a light, consistent hold on the handle and avoid letting slack build up, which can cause sudden jerks if the pet lunges.

How long can I expect the tape and spring to last with regular use?

Lifespan depends on frequency and conditions, but with routine cleaning and gentle storage the tape and spring typically hold up well for many months to a few years. Inspect the tape for fraying and the clip for secure attachment, and replace the leash if you notice significant wear.
